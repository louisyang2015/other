Implicants

an implicant --- any single 1 or any group of 1's which can be combined together on a map of the function F

prime implicant --- an implicant that cannot be combined with another term to eliminate a variable. (These are basically maxed out groups)

The yellow group is a prime implicant because it is a maxed out group that can not be expanded any further to eliminate variables. The other colors are not prime implicants because those groupings can be expanded further to eliminate variables.

essential  --- a minterm that is covered by only one prime implicant. This essential prime "owner" must be included in the minimum sum of products.
