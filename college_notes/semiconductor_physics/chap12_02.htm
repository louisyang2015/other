Transient (Switching) Response

The voltage quantities are such that when vs = -X * Vs , the transistor is in cutoff mode. Then, when vs = Vs , the transistor is in saturation mode.

The cutoff mode means iB = iC = iE = 0

In the saturation mode, the transistor is approximately a short circuit:

Using the above model for saturation, the currents in the "on" state are:

IBB = VS / RS
ICC = VCC / RL

After we left the off state (cutoff mode) and before we reach the on state (saturation mode), the BJT is in active mode.

The following graphs show what happens to the hole concentration in the base as we turn on and off the transistor.

  1. When BJT is turned off, the mode is cutoff. Both the EB and BC jucntions are in reverse bias, so the hole concentration is below equilibrium at the edge of the base region.
  2. As the BJT turns on, it is in active mode. The EB junction is in forward bias, so that end of the base region has excess minority carriers.
  3. As the BJT enters saturation, both EB and BC are in forward bias. Both ends of the base region gets excess minority carriers. We have the entire orange line moving upward when the BJT is in saturation.
  4. When the BJT is turning off, first the entire orange line drops straight down as the BJT leaves saturation and enters active mode.
  5. Then the excess charge in the EB junction is gradually drained until eventually the BJT is in the cutoff mode.

The behavior of iB :

When BJT is turned on, iB approach iBB very quickly. When the BJT is turned off, iB becomes a reverse bias current to discharge the excess minority carriers. This is just like what happens in the diode turned off response.

The behavior of iC :

In step 2, the iC increase rapidly at first. Then in step 3, the BJT enters saturation and the ∆PB line is mostly moving straight upward. iC is mostly the diffusion of holes across the base region, so if the slope of the ∆PB line is not changing much, iC would not change much.

In step 4, the BJT is turned off. The ∆PB line is at first moving straight down. Once again, the slope of ∆PB is not changing by much, so iC does not change much either. After step 4, the BJT leaves the saturation mode and goes through the active mode, the slope of ∆PB is changing rapidly and iC changes rapidly as well.
