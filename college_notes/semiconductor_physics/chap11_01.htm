Ideal Transistor Analysis

The ideal BJT transistor analysis is very similar to the ideal diode analysis. It's just that with the BJT there are two depletion regions instead of one.

Note: the following analysis applies to the pnp transistor only.

Notations:

Like in the ideal diode analysis, you first solve for the minority carriers.

The General Results (W arbitrary)

For the emitter and collector regions:

The ICn has a negative sign because we are reusing the ideal diode results, and those results assume a current flowing from the p junction to the n junction. ICn is flowing from the n base to the p collector.

For the base region we use the narrow base diode results, which are hyperbolic functions.

Under active mode biasing, the [e^(qVCB / kT) - 1] terms are small compared to the [e^(qVEB / kT) - 1] terms

IE = IEn + IEp

IC = ICn + ICp

 

Ebers-Moll Equations and Model

The equation representation for IE and IC would be large and unwieldy, but they are nevertheless listed on page 396 of the book. The Ebers-Moll model is a circuit representation of those equations.

IE = IF - αR IR

IC = αF IF - IR

IF = IF0 ( e^[(qVEB)/(kT)] - 1 )
IR = IR0 ( e^[(qVCB)/(kT)] - 1 )

The four parameters, αF , αR , IF0 , and IR0 can be deduced from a match to experimental data, or we can use these relationships to compute them:

αF IF0 = αR IR0
