Metal-Semiconductor Work function Difference

The convention in this chapter is:

  • Ф means energy level at a certain point in the MOS structure
  • ÿ means voltage over a certain interval of the MOS structure

ФM ≠ ФS = χ + (EC - EF)FB

The "FB" refers to the flat band region --- the bulk of the semiconductor that is far from the oxide interface.

The difference in metal-semiconductor work function creates built in band bending --- without an external application of VG. The insulator in the middle decreases both the metal and the semiconductor barriers by the same amount, so that ФM ‡ ФM' , and χ ‡ χ'.

Looking at the above diagram, the energy levels add up as:

ΦM' + q ΔΦox = (Ec - FF)FB - qÿS + χ'

Taking the metal to be zero potential, the built-in voltage is:

Vbi = -(ÿS + Δÿox) = ÿMS

where ∆ÿox is the voltage over the oxide and ÿs is the voltage over the section of the semiconductor near the oxide interface.

So the built-in potential inside a ФM ≠ ФS , but otherwise ideal, MOS structure is just the metal-semiconductor work-function difference expressed in volts.

This built in potential shows up as a shift in the voltage-capacitance graph.

The work function difference is dependent on the dopant level and the metal contact type.
