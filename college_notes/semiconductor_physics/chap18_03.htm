Threshold, Terminology, and Technology

An enhancement mode device is naturally off --- there is no channel at a gate bias of zero.

A depletion mode device is naturally on --- a non zero VG has to be applied to turn the channel off.

The problem with depletion mode devices is that an unwanted channel can very easily exist between two adjacent MOS devices --- because we have channels without applying power.

Up until around 1977, non-ideal factors tend to shift VT negative and forced NMOS devices to be depletion mode devices. This led to the total dominance of PMOS technology over NMOS. Eventually, dopant concentration control improved and NMOS then dominates PMOS because electrons have higher mobility than holes.

Actually, even for enhancement-mode devices, unwanted channels might still exist as long as the intermediate region has a VT that is similar to the MOSFET. The oxide layer of the MOSFET is called the gate oxide, while the oxide layer of the intermediate region is called field oxide. The real solution to unwanted is to have the field oxide be much thicker than the gate oxide so that the intermediate region has a larger |VT| than the device regions.

Back Biasing

Reverse biasing the substrate - source depletion region will affect VT . This is also known as the body effect, or the substrate-bias effect.

Say with the substrate grounded ØS = 2V will achieve inversion:

If a -1 volt is applied to the body, the 2V that will achieve inversion comes from source to oxide:

The voltage from oxide to the bulk is then 3 volts. In other words, VBS changes the inversion point in the semiconductor from 2ØF to 2ØF - VBS .

This 3 volts is not VT --- the 3 volts just goes from the bulk terminal to the semiconductor - oxide boundary. VT will include the voltage drop over the oxide in addition to the 3 volts.

The body effect changed the inversion ØS from 2V to 3V. The voltage drop over the oxide also changed. That is why VT(body effect) is not simply " VT(no body effect) + 1V ".

Likewise, the VGS'(at threshold) is not 2 volt --- VGS'(at threshold) is 2 volts plus certain number of volts across the oxide.
