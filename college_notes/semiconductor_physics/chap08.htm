ts = storage time, or storage delay time = the time the current remains constant during the switching

tr = recovery time = the difference between trr and ts

The diode is in forward bias during the time duration ts .

Because the decaying tr portion is readily distorted by stray capacitance in the measurement circuit, it is ts that has come to be quoted as the primary figure of merit in characterizing the turn-off transient.

Qualitative Analysis to Understand why is the Diode under Foward Bias

The circuit started out under forward bias and ultimately ends up as reverse bias. Under forward bias, there is an excess of minority carriers near the depletion region. During reverse bias there is a lack of them. So to go from forward bias to reverse bias, we must lose a number of the minority carriers.

As long as the number of minority carriers is above equilibrium at places near the depletion region, the diode is in forward bias. That is why during much of the transition to being turned off, the diode is in forward bias.

To get rid of the excess minority carriers, there is recombination and the reverse current. The reverse current basically takes the minority carriers across the depletion region. So although the diode is in forward bias, the minority carrier near depletion region is decreasing, and the current through the diode is in reverse direction.

Some ways to reduce ts

Small IF --- so fewer carriers to remove.

Large IR --- to remove carriers faster; but IR too large can damage the diode or enter the diode's breakdown region.

Smaller TP --- shorter carrier life time for faster recombination to remove the excess minority carriers. But Tp too small increases the reverse bias current IR-G by too much.

Some devices tries to have fewer stored carriers.

step recovery or snap-back diode --- these diodes have very small tr .
