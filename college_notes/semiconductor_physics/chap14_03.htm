Practical Contact Considerations

Rectifying Contacts

Contrary to the theory, ΦB is not ΦM - χ . The equilibrium Fermi level is pinned at specific energy levels, so that ΦB does not really depend on the metal used in the contact.

For example, regardless of the metal used, in GaAs MS diodes the ΦB is about 0.95 eV for n-type devices and 0.47 eV for p-type devices.

ΦB is measured and not determined from a prior knowledge of ΦM and χ.

Ohmic Contacts

Becuase of the Fermi level pinning, the deposistion of any metal on n-type GaAs forms a barrier-type contact. Therefore, ohmic contacts can not be formed by picking a particular metal so to have ΦM < ΦS (n-type semiconductors attached to metal).

Ohmic contacts are really formed by heavily doping the semiconductor near the MS contact.

Heavy doping does not reduce the height of the energy barrier, but it narrows the barrier region so that carriers can tunnel through the barrier.

At doping levels exceeding 1019 cm-3, the entire barrier becomes so narrow and carriers can tunnel through so easily that it's as if an ohmic contact is formed.

Silicon devices routinely contain a thin oxide layer between the metal and the semiconductor, because a native oxide layer will form almost instantly when silicon is exposed to the atmosphere. This introduces resistance. Annealing or heating the device structure is routinely necessary to minimize the contact resistance.

Aluminum is the most widely used contacting metal. Heating to approximately 475 oC in a nitrogen atmosphere allows aluminum to penetrate the native oxide layer on the Si surface. The non-uniform penetration of Al into Si is called spiking:

Aluminum is not temperature stable. If additional processing at temperatures in excess of 500 oC must be performed after contact formation, then aluminum can not be used as the contact material. Titanium can be used instead. A Ti film deposited over the Si contact area will mix with the Si to form TiSi2. This TiSi2 will move only a short distance into the Si, and the result is a clean, planar, temperature-stable, ohmic contact.
