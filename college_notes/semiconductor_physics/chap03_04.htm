Minority Carrier Diffusion Equations

The continuity equations can be further simplifies if the following seven conditions are true:

  1. The system is one dimensional. The "n" or "p" varies with just "x".
  2. The analysis is done on minority carries --- so the "n" or "p" are minority carriers within a semiconductor.
  3. The electric field, E, ≈ 0 inside the semiconductor.
  4. The equilibrium minority carrier concentrations are not a function of position. Instead, n0 or p0 is a constant throughout the semiconductor.
  5. Low level injection conditions prevail. That is, let ∆p = p - p0 , ∆n = n - n0 .  " n " and " p " are minority carriers, and ∆p and ∆n are small compare to the majority carrier concentration.
  6. Indirect thermal recombination-generation is the dominant thermal R-G mechanism.
  7. There are no "other processes", except possibly photogeneration, taking place within the system.

For the problems in the book for this section, these seven conditions are true. The continuity equations then simplify to:

The subscripts are added to remind the user that the equations are valid only for the minority carriers. For example, np means that we are talking about the free electrons in a p-type semiconductor.

Note the equations are about ∆p or ∆n, not " p " or " n ". When doing problems, don't think about the actual number of " p " or " n ", but think about how far the " p " or " n " is away from equilibrium.

Diffusion Lengths

In example problem 2, we introduced LP where:

LP = (DP Tp)1/2

Similarly we have:

LN = (DN Tn)1/2

LP , LN represent the average distance minority carriers can diffuse into a sea of majority carriers before being annihilated. These are called minority carrier diffusion lengths. In example problem 2, diffusion lengths are the average position of the excess minority carriers.

 

Quasi-Fermi Levels

Under equilibrium, the Fermi Energy level and the carrier concentration is related by:

n0 = ni e^[(EF - Ei) / (kT)]
p0 = ni e^[(Ei - EF) / (kT)]

In order to have "look alike" equations for the non-equilibrium cases, we invent FN and FP such that:

n = ni e^[(FN - Ei) / (kT)]
p = ni e^[(Ei - FP) / (kT)]

Finally, quasi-Fermi energy level relates to current density:

JP = μp p (sFP)
JN = μn n (sFN)

So a diagram showing a quasi-Fermi level that varies with position indicates that current is flowing inside the semiconductor.
