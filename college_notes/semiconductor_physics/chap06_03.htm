So ∆p(x') is a line if the quasineutral region is very short. This linear ∆p(x') is the same result that we would get if we ignore thermal recombination-generation to begin with. This shows that we can neglect the thermal R-G term in the minority carrier diffusion equation when the quasineutral width is small compared to the diffusion length.

The equation for I0' depends on xc'. Under reverse bias, xc' decreases with increasingly negative VA . So I0' does not saturate under reverse bias --- while the I0 for the ideal diode equation is a saturation current.

Under sufficient reverse bias, the depletion region can expand such that it consumes the entire n junction such that xc - xn = xc' = 0. This situation is called punch-through. The current equations above says that as xc' à 0, IDIFF (VA < 0) à -∞. That is of course not correct, so the above current equations do not apply in punch-through situations. Experimentally, punch-through situations leads to saturated current, until we reach voltage breakdown that is.
