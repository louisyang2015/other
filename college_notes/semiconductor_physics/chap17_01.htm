MOSFET I-V Relationships

Qualitative Theory of Operations

Which is source and which is drain? --- Current flow is always such that carriers (electrons in NMOS) enter the structure through the source (S) and leave through the drain (D).

Normal operation picture:

Note that the drain (VD ≥ 0) and the substrate (VB = 0) forms a reverse bias.

When VG is accumulation or depletion biased, the substrate near the SiO2-Si boundary has very few electrons and ID is nearly zero. When only when VG is inversion biased, the n+ islands will see an "n-type" region between them and we will get a current ID.

There can be a small leak current that goes from drain (D) to bulk (B) --- instead of drain to source.

Assume a channel is formed, when VD is small and we step up VD , the channel acts like a simple resistor. Eventually, when VD gets large enough, the VD will start to negate the inverting effect of VG . Near the drain region, the voltage that determines whether the channel will invert is VGD . As VD increase, the channel near the drain shrinks.

The dash line marks the depletion region between the n regions on top and the p substrate.

Eventually, when VD = VDSat , the channel pinches off entirely. At this point, the current ID should saturate (almost).

A picture of pinch-off:

When we increase VD beyond VDSat , the channel no longer extends from source to drain. Instead, a depletion region now disrupts the channel.

We now have the same voltage drop VDSat over a channel that is shorter. This will increase current. If the channel is long (L >> ∆L), we won't see much current increase. If the channel is short enough, we will continue to see a current increase with increasing VD .

From the origin to A the channel is acting like a resistor. From A to B the current starts to saturate.

VD < VDSat is called the linear or triode region.
VD > VDSat is called the saturation region.

Comparing bulk-charge and square law theories:

The bulk-charge theory's ID is always smaller than the square law theory's ID. The bulk-charge theory is closer to the square law theory for lower substrate dopings.

Problems with the bulk-charge theory:

  • When VD ≥ VDSat the current does not saturate but keeps on increasing
  • When VG ≤ VT , the charge in the channel (QN) is assumed to be zero in the bulk-charge theory --- but in reality QN is not zero --- there's a minor current even if VG ≤ VT .
