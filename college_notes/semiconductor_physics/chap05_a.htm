How to Analyze pn Junction Electrostatics

Energy Band diagram for pn Junction

A p type semiconductor has the Fermi energy level drawn closer to the valence band, while an n type semiconductor has the Fermi energy level drawn closer to the conductor band.

For a pn junction under equilibrium, the Fermi energy is constant throughout the semiconductor. So for  the combined band diagram, we match the EF levels.

The pn Junction and the Depletion Region

The following is a picture of the pn junction.

Near the border between the p and n junctions, thermal agitation will free the electron from the donors and give them to the acceptors.

The border between the p and n junction is called the depletion region because the carriers are lost due to recombination --- the dopants near the border has formed the covalent bonds they wanted.

On the p side there is a build up of negative charge because the acceptors acquired electrons. On the n side there is a build up of positive charge because the donors gave up their electrons. The presence of excess charge leads to an electric field (E) and a built in potential (Vbi).

In the above picture we marked the extent of the depletion region with the varibles (-xp) and (xn).

Charge Density (ρ) of the Depletion Region

Note that previously we used the symbol (ρ) for resistivity. In this section (ρ) is for charge density.

Charge density is charge per cm3 , and we can get this density by adding up all the charges in the depletion region. Assuming the dopants to be totally ionized:

ρ = q(p - n + ND - NA)

p = holes per cm3
n = free electrons per cm3
ND = donors per cm3
NA = acceptors per cm3

The Depletion Approximation --- This is the assumption that there is total ionization in the depletion region, and then all of the carriers in the depletion region have disappeared due to recombination. Also, outside of the depletion region the excess charge is zero.

xp and xn

There is no universal way to get xp and xn . These numbers are the result of boundary conditions imposed on the voltage (V), and also a result of the total area under the charge density graph be zero.

The depletion width is xp + xn .

 

An applied Voltage VA

For this chapter, it is assumed that all of the applied voltage drops across the depletion region. So applying VA just changes Vbi .

Note the polarity of the set up. Vbi wants to push holes from the n region to the p region, while VA wants to push holes from the p region to the n region --- so Vbi and VA works against each other. The presence of VA changes Vbi to (Vbi - VA)

A positive VA narrows the depletion region.
