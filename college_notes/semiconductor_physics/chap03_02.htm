Carrier Diffusion

The Concept of Diffusion

Thermal energy cause the carriers in a semiconductor to move around randomly. Diffusion is the tendency of the carriers to spread such that every place in a semiconductor gets an equal concentration of carriers.

Hot-Point Probe Measurement --- This is an experiment to test whether a semiconductor is n-type or p-type.

When a hot probe contacts a semiconductor, the heat produces carriers at the point of contact. The carrier density is higher at the point of contact than elsewhere, so the carriers at the point of contact diffuse away, creating a current that can be detected.

 

Non-Uniform Doping

Here is a semiconductor that has higher doping on the right side than the left side:

Thermal energy will free the extra electron of donor atoms. Diffusion will cause this extra electron to migrate from the right side to the left side. Yet, as soon as the right side lose an electron to the left side, the right side gains a positive charge due to the nucleus of the donor atom. The left side gains a negative charge as a result of the incoming electron. This scenario will generate an electric field that acts to push the extra electron back to the right side.

So a non-uniformly doped semiconductor, at room temperature, will have a diffusion current, a built in electric field, and a drift current that counters the diffusion current.

For the problems, whenever the energy band diagram shows a constant EF , assume the semiconductor is in equilibrium conditions.

If an equation is given for ND , and we assume total ionization such that n = ND , we can compute an equation for EC by using the relationship from chapter 2:

The Diffusion current in Numerical Terms

Let:

JP|diff = diffusion current of the holes
JN|diff = diffusion current of the electrons
p = holes per cm3 and it varies as a function of position
n = electrons per cm3 and it varies as a function of position

JP|diff = -q DP sp
JN|diff = q DN sn

DP and DN are special numbers called hole and electron diffusion coefficients. Their unit is cm2 / sec.

In one dimensional cases, sp becomes dp/dx, and sn becomes dn/dx.

 

The total current going through a semiconductor is the sum of the diffusion current and the drift current.

Let:

JP = the current due to holes
JN = the current due to electrons
μ = carrier mobility
E = electric field exerted on the carrier

JP = q μp p E - q DP sp
JN = q μn n E + q DN sn

In a non-uniformly doped semiconductor under equilibrium,  JP = 0, and JN = 0. These requirements then leads to a relationship between the coefficient for diffusion and the carrier mobility.
