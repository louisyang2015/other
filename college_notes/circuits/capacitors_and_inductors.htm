Beyond the basics, the capacitor and inductors are duels --- because their basic current-voltage relationships are described by duel functions. The capacitor equations are on the left side, employing the capacitance value (C) and inductor equations are on the right side, employing the inductance value (L).

The above equations shows that in DC steady state (when v(t), i(t) stops changing):

  • the capacitor gets zero current (it acts as an open circuit)
  • the inductor gets zero voltage (it acts as a short circuit)
