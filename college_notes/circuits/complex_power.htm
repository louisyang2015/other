Complex Power

The basic definition of complex power (S):

S = Vrms * (conjugate of Irms)

In polar form:

S = |Vrms| * |Irms| / θv - θi

The real part of complex power is the average power. The imaginary part of complex power is called quadrature power.

Another expression for complex power:

Z * |Irms|2 = S

The following example is done two ways, first without complex power, and then with complex power. Personally, I think complex power is usually not needed.

Power Factor Correction Example:

The complex power (SL) of the load by itself:

cos (θv - θi) = 0.8

SL = 62.5 * 103 / 36.87o

The new θv - θi that we want is:

cos (θv - θi) = 0.95
θv - θi = 18.195o
