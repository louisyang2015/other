Once you convert the components to their frequency domain versions, you can use circuit analysis techniques to get VO(s) or whatever it is that you need. Then you do an inverse transform to get vo(t).

The Laplace transform has no "knowledge" of what goes on before time zero. The resulting expression for vo(t) always has a term "u(t)" attached to it because that expression is not valid for time < 0. Another side affect is that both of the following voltage forms will have a Laplace transform of 10/s.

The two different wave forms can create different values for the voltage/current of capacitor/inductor at time zero. So although the Laplace transform on a voltage source would treat both of these wave forms equally, the Laplace transform on the capacitor/inductor portions of the circuit might produce different results.
