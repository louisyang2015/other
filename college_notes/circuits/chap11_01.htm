This is a driving point function --- A driving point function describes the impedance or admittance defined at a pair of terminals.

An equation in terms of " s " that describes the input and output of a circuit is called a transfer function.

Example:

" Vo(s) / Vs(s) " would be the transfer function describing the gain.

To get the transfer function describing the gain, you usually use intermediate variables. In this case, get an expression relating Vs(s) and Vin(s), a second expression relating Vin(s) to Vo(s). Then you put the two expressions together to get " Vo(s) / Vs(s) "

The transfer functions and the driving point functions have this general format:

H(s) =
K0 (s - z1) (s - z2) ... (s - zn)

The p's are called poles. The z's are called zeros. The poles and zeros can be complex --- but if they are complex, they must appear in conjugate pairs because the original polynomial is made of real coefficients.
