Bode Plots --- Magnitude Characteristic

In the magnitude plot portion of bode plots, the vertical axis is in decibels.

About decibels

Decibel (dB) is used to describe the ratio of powers:

number of dB = 10 * log10
P1
P2

power is directly proportional to the square of voltage or current, so:

number of dB = 10 * log10
|V1 |
|V2 |
number of dB = 10 * log10
|I1 |
|I2 |

So the y-value for the bode magnitude plot is 20 * log10 (magnitude of a function).

The Bode Magnitude Graph is a Sum of Terms

Say we are given the transfer function:

Gv(jw) =
10*(1 + 0.1jw)
(1 + jw) (1 + 0.02jw)

The magnitude is:

|Gv(jw)| =
10*|1 + 0.1jw|
|1 + jw| |1 + 0.02jw|

The y-value of the Bode Magnitude graph is 20 * log10 (|Gv(jw)|):

20 * log10 |Gv(jw)| = 20*log10 10 + 20*log10 |1 + 0.1jw| - 20*log10 |1 + jw| - 20*log10 |1 + 0.02jw|

If we know how the graph for each term will look like, then we will know how the graph for the whole thing will look like.

 

The Graph for a Single Term

The function:

y = 20 log10 |1 + p * wj|,
where p is some positive number

has the following approximation:

w approximate y exact y
(1 / p) / 100 0 0.00043
(1 / p) / 10 0 0.04321
1 / p 0 3.0103
10 / p 20 20.043
100 / p 40 40

Note that at w = 1/p, the real graph bends 3 dB above the approximation --- this w is known as the break frequency or the 3dB frequency.

Also note that (1 + p * wj) and (1 - p * wj) has the same magnitude.

Summary

The term:

(1 + p * wj)

if in the numerator, it means bending upward at a rate of 20 dB per decade starting at the frequency w = 1/p. If in the denominator, it means bending downward at a rate of 20 dB per decade starting at the frequency w = 1/p.

The term:

(wj)

if in the numerator, it means that the graph's starting slope is +20 dB per decade. If in the denominator, it means the starting slope is -20 dB per decade.

The term:

(1 + B*(jw) + (p * jw)2)

if in the numerator, it means bending upward at a rate of 40 dB per decade starting at the frequency w = 1/p. In addition, the exact value at w = 1/p should be numerically evaluated. If in the denominator, it means bending downward at a rate of 40 dB per decade starting at the frequency w = 1/p.

The above observations tell you which way the graph bends. To have an actual graph, you should pick a small value of " w ", calculate the magnitude of the function at that value, and use this (w, magnitude) as a starting point.
