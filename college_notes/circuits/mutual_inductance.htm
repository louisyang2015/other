An inductor can not store negative energy --- so the above expression means that there is an upper bound for M. If M is too large, w(t) will become negative --- which is not possible in real life.

It turns out that:

M ≤ (L1 L2)1/2

To prove that M ≤ (L1 L2)1/2 , use the inequality:

	(a - b)2 ≥ 0
	a2 + b2 ≥ 2ab
	(1/2)a2 + (1/2)b2 ≥ ab
	a matches up with (L1)1/2 i1(t)
	b matches up with (L2)1/2 i2(t)
	and so M matches up with L1L2

k = 0 means no coupling and no mutual inductance
