Three-Phase Circuits

Following is a balanced three-phase circuit :

  • The three voltages are of the same magnitude but 120o out of phase with each other
  • The three currents are also of the same magnitude but 120o out of phase with each other
  • While it's not drawn, it's implied that the three loads have equal impedance so to produce the balanced current.

Conventions used by this documentation:

  Time Domain Phasor Rms Notation
The three voltage sources: van(t) = Vm cos(wt) V
vbn(t) = Vm cos(wt - 120o) V
vcn(t) = Vm cos(wt - 240o) V		 Van = Vm / 0o
Vbn = Vm / -120o
Vcn = Vm / -240o		 Van-rms = |Vrms| / 0o
Vbn-rms = |Vrms| / -120o
Vcn-rms = |Vrms| / -240o
The three currents: ia(t) = Im cos(wt - θ) A
ib(t) = Im cos(wt - θ - 120o) A
ic(t) = Im cos(wt - θ - 240o) A		 Ia = Im / -θ
Ib = Im / -θ - 120o
Ic = Im / -θ - 240o		 Ia-rms = |Irms| / -θ
Ib-rms = |Irms| / -θ - 120o
Ic-rms = |Irms| / -θ - 240o

Due to the pattern that exist, for a balanced circuit, it's only necessary to figure out one phase: say Van and ia , then the remaining numbers will be clear.

Also:

  • Van + Vbn + Vcn = 0
  • Ia + Ib + Ic = 0
