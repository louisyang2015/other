For a general impedance Z = R + jX, we can picture it as a resistor in series with an inductor/capacitor. The power absorbed by this impedance Z is the power absorbed by the resistor R. So to compute the power absorbed by Z, we do not need to have both the phasor V across Z and the phasor I through Z --- we just need the phasor I through Z, or the phasor V across R.

Superposition is not applicable to power because p(t) is not linearly related to solely v(t) or solely i(t).
