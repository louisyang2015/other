Transient Analysis of Second Order Circuits

Second order circuits have two storage elements. These circuits are solved by solving a second order differential equation.

A second order homogeneous differential equation has the form:

A
d2 x(t)
dt2
+ B
d x(t)
dt
+ C x(t) = 0

where A, B, C are constants. We solve this equation by solving the characteristic equation:

	As2 + Bs + C = 0

The roots of the characteristic equation, r1 and r2, determines the complementary solution.

Three Forms of the Complementary Solution

If r1 and r2 are both real,

	xc(t) = K1 er1t + K2 er2t

and this case is called over damped.

If r1 and r2 are the same,

	xc(t) = (K1 + k2 t)er1t

and this case is called critically damped.

If r1 and r2 are complex conjugates,

	r1 = a + bi
	r2 = a - bi
	xc(t) = eax(A1 * cos bt + A2 * sin bt)

and this case is called under damped

An under damped graph is an oscillation compressed by a eax envelope.

An example of under damped graph:

	xc(t) = e-x(cos 10t + sin 10t)

Both the overly damped case and the critically damped case can have a rise and fall effect. It's not possible to compare the two cases directly. The reason is that overly damped case can achieve a rise and fall effect by exploiting the difference between two different esomething terms, while the critically damped case only use one esomething term.

	xc(t) = 3 e-0.5t - 2 e-2t (blue line)
	xc(t) = (1 + 2t) e-0.5t (green line)
	xc(t) = (1 + 2t) e-2t (red line)
	xc(t) = (1 + 2t) e-1.25t (blue green line)

The blue line is overly damped while all three others are critically damped. Some books (including the book that this documentation is based on) will say that critically damped will rise to peak and decline to steady state faster than overly damped --- this is NOT true in the sense that not all critically damped curves will approach steady state faster than all overly damped curves. How fast the curve peak or approach zero has to do with starting value and the time constant for decay.

d2 x(t)
dt
+ 2sw
d x(t)
dt
+ w2 x(t) = 0

s --- is called the exponential damping ratio

w --- is called the undamped natural frequency

s > 1 means xc(t) is over damped
s = 1 means xc(t) is critically damped
s < 1 means xc(t) is under damped
