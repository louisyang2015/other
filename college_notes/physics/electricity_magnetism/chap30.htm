Solenoid

If the solenoid is long and narrow, the magnetic field in the middle is approximately uniform within the solenoid, and zero outside of the solenoid.

So if do a line integral of the B field on sides 1, 2, 3, 4, the B field can be assumed to be zero on sides 2, 3, 4, (this is for the long, narrow, "ideal" solenoids).

B * L = μ0 * (N * I)

where L is the length of side 3 and over the interval L there are N turns, each carrying current I.

B = μ0 n I

where n = N / L is the number of turns per unit length.

Magnetic Flux (Φ)

ΦB = ∫ B  dA

If we have a plane area A, and a uniform magnetic field B that makes an angle θ with the plane, then
ΦB = BA cos θ .

Guass's Law for Magnetism: the net magnetic flux through any closed surface is always zero.

ΦB = ∫closed B  dS = 0

 

Generalized Form of Ampere's Law

Because it was Maxwell who generalized Ampere's original law, the generalized Ampere's Law is also known as the Ampere-Maxwell law.

The problem for the original Ampere's law is that in between the plates of a capacitor, there is no current flow --- so the magnetic field there is zero if we use the older Ampere's law. Yet, as long as charges are accumulating on the plates of a capacitor, there is a magnetic field. So it's not just a current that generates a magnetic field, a changing electric field can also generate a magnetic field.

Maxwell decided to take the changing electric field into account by making it a "displacement current" (Id) .

Magnetic field of the Earth

The south magnetic pole of the Earth is about 1,300 miles away from the geographical north pole. The north magnetic pole is about 1,200 miles away from the geographical south pole. So things are flipped and they are not exact.

The source of Earth's magnetic field is NOT a bar of magnet. The temperature inside Earth makes it impossible. Instead, Earth's magnetic field comes from internal electric currents.

Planets with faster rotations seem to have stronger magnetic fields. Jupiter's magnetic field is stronger than ours while Venus's is weaker.

The direction of field has be reversed several times during the last million years. The evidence comes from volcanic rocks that solidified.
