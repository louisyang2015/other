A Particle in a Box

The particle in a box scenario is to have a particle, moving on the x-axis, bouncing back and forth between two barriers that have infinite potential energy. The particle is trapped between the two walls and can not go beyond the walls.

The classical analogy is to have a string whose ends are tied to two walls. To support a standing wave between two walls, there must be nodes held fixed at the ends.

When the particle move from E3 to E2 , it will release photons. In reverse, when the particle at level E2 absorb (E3 - E2) Joule of energy from a photon, the particle will move to E3.
