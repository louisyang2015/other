Bohr's Quantum Model of the Atom

Bohr's model is a hybrid of basic Quantum principles and classical mechanics.

The electron moves in a circular orbit around the proton, under the influence of the Coulomb force of attraction.

n = 1 is called the ground state, n = 2 is called the first excited state, and so on.

The ground state is as negative as En can get. This is the state that will require the most energy to free an electron from influence of the proton.

The ionization energy is the minimum energy required to ionize the atom --- to completely remove an electron in the ground state from the proton's influence.

For the hydrogen atom, E1 is the ionization energy.
