Ionic Bonds

NaCl = Sodium Chloride

Na's electron configuration: 1s22s22p63s
Cl's electron configuration: 1s22s22p5

so Na gives one electron to Cl and the two ions attract

The energy released when an atom takes on an electron is called electron affinity. For chlorine, the electron affinity is 3.6 eV.

The energy required to ionize the Na atom into Na+ is 5.1 eV.

Therefore, if two neutral atoms Cl and Na are separated by large distances, an energy of (5.1 - 3.6) eV must be provided to the neutral atoms to change them into Na+ and Cl- ions.

The energy it takes to separate a molecule into neutral atoms is called the dissociation energy.

U0 is called the ionic cohesive energy of the solid. For NaCl, it is -7.84 eV per Na+ - Cl- pair. So we need to supply 7.84 eV to separate a Na+ - Cl- ionic bond into two separate ions.

Then going from Na+ to Na will release 5.14 eV, but going from Cl- to Cl+ will require 3.61 eV. So to go from a Na+ - Cl- ionic bond to two neutral atoms will require (7.84 - 5.14 + 3.61 = 6.31) eV. This 6.31 eV is called the atomic cohesive energy, which is the energy difference between the ionic bond and two neutral atoms.
