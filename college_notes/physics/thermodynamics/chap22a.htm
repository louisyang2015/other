Heat Engines and Heat Pumps

Schematic representation of a heat engine:

So that Qh = W + Qc , all terms are positive.

In real life terms, Qh is the heat provided by burning some fuel. W is the work done by an engine. Qc is the heat lost to cooling the engine. We purposely cool the engine by letting out the hot air within the engine, so that Qc is the heat escaped through the exhaust. The engine can be cooled by the colder temperature surrounding the engine.

Heat Pump

Heat pump is just a heat engine in reverse:

In real life terms, Qc is the heat taken away from the food inside a refrigerator, W is the electric power supplied to the refrigerator, and Qh is the heat expelled in the coils on the back of the refrigerator.

The Second Law of Thermal Dynamics (Clausius statement) --- It is impossible to construct a heat pump with an effiency of 100%

A heat pump can either be a heater or a refrigerator, depending on which reservoir we focus on. If the goal is to heat something, we say the heat pump is in heating mode.
