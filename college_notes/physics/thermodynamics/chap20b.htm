energy reservoir --- this is a conceptual object that can maintain a certain temperature level. Say an object is an energy reservoir at 30oC. No matter how much hot water or cold water we pour on the object, it is still at 30oC and will make the water touching it 30oC. It is as if the object can accept infinite energy or give infinite energy.

 

The First Law of Thermodynamics

ΔEint = Q - W

∆Eint --- change in the internal energy of the system
Q --- heat going into the system
W --- work done by the system

Note:

  • Work done by the system reduces internal energy. Positive work is equivalent to energy leaving the system
  • Q and W are path dependent --- they depend on the pressure (P) versus volume (V) graph. But ∆Eint is a state variable and it depends only on the end point. Therefore, Q - W depends only on the end point of the system's states.
  • For a thermodynamic system, the internal energy is determined by the state of the system, and the internal energy function is therefore called a state function.

Cyclic process --- we start and end at the same state. ∆Eint = 0, Q = W. The net work done by the system per cycle equals the area enclosed by the path representing the process on a PV diagram.

adiabatic process --- no energy enters or leaves the system by heat. Q = 0, ∆Eint = -W.

adiabatic free expansion --- It's the adiabatic case plus the requirement that the initial and final internal energies of a gas are equal. So Q = 0, ∆Eint = -W = 0

isobaric process --- pressure held constant. The equation for work is simplified to W = P( Vfinal - Vstart )

isovolumetric process --- volume held constant. W = 0, ∆Eint = Q. In these cases, if energy is added by heat to a system kept at constant volume, then all of the transferred energy remains in the system as an increase of the internal energy of the system.

isothermal process --- temperature held constant. For an ideal gas, the internal energy is a function of temperature only. ∆Eint = 0, Q = W.

Energy Transfer Mechanisms (How Heat Spreads)

thermal conduction --- this is when heat spreads through the exchange of kinetic energy between atomic level particles. Example: heat spreading from one end of a metal bar to another.

Metal is a good conductor of heat because they contain large number of relatively free electrons. In a good heat conductor, conduction takes place both by means of the vibration of atoms and by means of motion of free electrons.

convection --- energy transferred by the movement of a heated substance. When the movement results from differences in density, as with hot air around a fire that ended up rising upward, it is referred to as natural convection.

I think conduction is about heat spreading through a solid, and convection is about heat spreading through a liquid or gas.

radiation --- all objects radiate energy continuously in the form of electromagnetic waves.

Numerical Description of Radiation

Stefan's Law

P = σ A e T4

P --- power in watts radiated by the object
σ --- a constant = 5.6696 * 10-8 W / (m2 K4)
A --- surface area of the object in square meters
e --- emissivity constant, between 1 and 0
T --- surface temperature in kelvins

An object with temperature T, within a surrounding temperature T0, would be both absorbing and radiating energy:

P = σ A e (T4 - T04)

(e) is between 1 and 0. It is the fraction of the incoming radiation that the surface absorbs.

ideal absorber --- also called a black body --- has (e) = 1. This also maximizes radiation

ideal reflector --- has (e) = 0. Such object reflects all energy incident on it, and there is no radiation.
